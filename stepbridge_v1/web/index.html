User Guide

StepBridge is a CV/Pulse/MIDI step sequencer for the Music Thing Modular Workshop Computer.

Panel controls

  • Down (Utility): Main knob = sequence length, Y knob = track select.
  • Middle (Step Edit): Main knob = pitch of the scrubbed step (turn fully down for rest), X knob = step scrub, Y knob = gate length of the current step.
  • Up (Tempo/Bank): Main knob = tempo (only while using the internal clock), X = slot select, Y = save/load trigger.

Clock

Patch a clock signal into Pulse In 1 to sync to an external clock; leave it unpatched to use the internal tempo knob. Pulse In 2 resets all tracks to step 1.

Pressing Play arms the sequencer rather than guaranteeing an instant start. With External Pulse or MIDI Clock selected, playback genuinely can't begin until the next real pulse/tick arrives from the external clock — this is standard external-clock behavior, not a bug. You'll see "Armed…" (pulsing) until the first pulse actually arrives.

Web UI

Click a step in the Sequencer tab to set its pitch, or click near the bottom of a step to make it a rest. Use the transport buttons to stop, pause, or play the sequencer from the browser.

CV routing and calibration live in the System tab and are now saved to flash — they survive power cycles and reflashes.

Track limit: 4 tracks maximum

StepBridge is hard-capped at 4 tracks. This is a confirmed hardware constraint of the RP2040 Workshop Computer, found through testing during development:

  • The four panel knobs (Main, X, Y, Switch) share a single RP2040 ADC channel through a 2-bit hardware multiplexer, cycling at 48 kHz (12 kHz per knob).
  • At 5+ tracks, knob telemetry becomes completely unstable — all four knob readings jitter simultaneously, knobs become unresponsive, and the state persists even after removing tracks. At 4 tracks, everything is rock solid.
  • Crucially, CV1 and CV2 also become unresponsive at 5+ tracks, not just the mux-shared panel knobs. This rules out ADC mux crosstalk as the root cause — the failure is systemic, affecting all ADC inputs simultaneously.
  • The most likely cause is USB/ADC interrupt contention: the MSG_TRACK_STEPS burst for a new 5th track (~211 bytes) may disrupt the 48 kHz ComputerCard ISR's ADC sampling timing badly enough that the entire ADC subsystem stops producing valid readings. The failure being permanent until power-cycle suggests corrupted ISR/DMA state rather than simple timing jitter.

Challenge: can you push past 4 tracks?

If you're a developer and want to investigate the 5-track instability, here's what we know:

  • The instability appears immediately when the 5th track is added via MSG_ADD_TRACK, before any knob is touched — suggesting it's triggered by the USB burst (MSG_TRACK_STEPS + MSG_NUM_TRACKS) rather than an audio-path timing issue.
  • Increasing the MIDI TX buffer from 64 to 512 bytes (CFG_TUD_MIDI_TX_BUFSIZE in tusb_config.h) eliminated the SysEx frame corruption at 5 tracks but did not fix the knob instability.
  • The 4-track limit holds even after a power cycle — the instability isn't a transient USB state issue. Removing tracks from 5 back to 4 does not recover stability; a full power cycle is required.
  • Potential avenues: rate-limiting or chunking MSG_TRACK_STEPS sends on add (spread the burst over multiple tud_task() iterations), investigating whether TinyUSB's interrupt handler on core0 can preempt the ComputerCard ADC ISR on core1 long enough to corrupt DMA/ADC state, or checking whether the RP2040's ADC DMA channel is sharing an IRQ with the USB stack.

Contributions welcome — this is an open hardware/firmware project. The source is in the stepbridge/ directory of the Workshop Computer repository.

Pull model: every request gets either a data response or an explicit NACK; a request with no reply within 750ms is logged as a timeout. MSG_PLAYHEAD is the only unprompted broadcast.

Note: every SysEx data byte, including the command byte, must be 0-127. Host→Firmware IDs were originally specced at 0x60-0x89 and renumbered to 0x50-0x79 after this was caught during milestone-3 hardware testing (0x85 is 133, an illegal MIDI status byte value).

MIDI Clock: firmware honors incoming realtime Start(0xFA)/Continue(0xFB)/Stop(0xFC) as transport commands and 0xF8 (24 PPQN) for step timing, but only once MIDI Clock is the explicitly selected source.

CV routing and calibration are persisted to flash automatically (no slot save needed). The FlashStore magic number was bumped to 0x42535054 when these fields were added — old saves were invalidated.
